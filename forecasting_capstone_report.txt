CAPSTONE PROJECT 3: SALES / DEMAND FORECASTING
==================================================

1. TIME-BASED DATA ANALYSIS
----------------------------------------
Date range: 2024-01-01 to 2025-12-30 (730 days, no gaps)

2. TREND AND SEASONALITY
----------------------------------------
Trend: sales grew from about 570/day to about 1011/day over the period.
Sunday is consistently the strongest sales day.
A seasonal demand bump also appears in the Oct-Nov window.

3. FORECASTING MODEL COMPARISON
----------------------------------------
Test period: last 60 days (time-ordered split, not random).

Seasonal Naive (baseline): MAE=136.95, RMSE=158.99, MAPE=13.87%
Moving Average (14-day): MAE=141.97, RMSE=167.46, MAPE=14.47%
Holt-Winters Exponential Smoothing: MAE=158.32, RMSE=183.33, MAPE=16.07%
Linear Regression (trend + day-of-week): MAE=86.19, RMSE=113.78, MAPE=7.88%

Best model: Linear Regression (trend + day-of-week) (MAE = 86.19)
This beats the baseline by 37.1%, a real improvement.

4. BUSINESS IMPACT ANALYSIS
----------------------------------------
Average daily forecast error (best model): 86.19 units.
At $8.5 profit per unit, that is roughly $733 per day in over or under stocking risk.
Annualized, about $267404 per year in residual inventory cost that forecasting alone cannot remove.
Value of switching from the baseline to the best model: about $157483 per year saved.

RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Plan extra staff and stock for Sundays, it is a structural pattern.
- Build in extra stock ahead of the Oct-Nov seasonal window.
- Use Linear Regression (trend + day-of-week) for forecasting, but keep the baseline as a check.
  If the model error goes above the baseline error, the pattern has likely shifted.
